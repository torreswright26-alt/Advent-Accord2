# Manifestation System

**Status:** Canon

Every manifestation has a deeply personal identity defined by its evolving **medium**. The medium attempts to take shape rather than accepting a fixed imposed shape. It follows motion and intent when it succeeds.

## States

- **Dormant:** no manifestation effects.
- **Active:** may curl around or move with the user; alter appearance, clothing, posture, expression, or silhouette; affect nearby material, atmosphere, or space; operate through contact or a zone.

Direct contact is required only when the use is inherently contact- or weapon-based.

## Quality, strength, and mastery

Quality reflects mind, heart, intent, and conviction—not strength. Unresolved expression may be cloudy or soft-edged; aligned intent may be crisp or clear. Clarity is not complexity, size, or decorative detail.

Mastery has no single ideal. Harmony rewards authenticity, creativity, intent, understanding, and meaningful user-medium integration. Simple and elaborate expressions can both represent mastery.

## Progression

Beginners often begin generalized, depersonalized, or hint-like, though some start more completely formed. Progression reveals personal specificity rather than only spectacle. Aspects can be used independently or combined.

## Functional range

Manifestations may support utility, perception, mobility, defense, craft, communication, environmental control, combat, and other identity-consistent functions. They must not default to spears or weapons when other expressions are more fitting.

## Expression framework

Dormant; First Activation; Utility; Defensive; Offensive; Maximum; Out of Control; Exhaustion; Residue. These are distinct questions, not mandatory linear power levels.

Damage or exhaustion may appear through fractures, leaking material or energy, instability, incomplete motion, bodily changes, or residue—but must belong to the medium. Loss of control need not produce catastrophic destruction.

## Prohibitions

Unless intrinsic, avoid crystalline facets, arbitrary geometry, weapon defaults, monochromatic attack matching, excessive particles, clutter-as-power, and generic explosions.

## Sol

Sol's manifestation is smoky and painterly, with smoke dominant, using red-orange with silver streaks. Sol retains correct anatomy and never merges with his scythe. Early scythe expression contains swirling smoke; mature expression shows intentional scythe presence without body-merge.
