# World and Locations

Every civilization and important location must feel authored through culture, environment, history, materials, technology, population, and use.

Avoid generic medieval Europe, JRPG fantasy, cyberpunk, East Asian fantasy, and empty monumental architecture.

## Required logic

- geography and climate
- local and imported materials
- architectural silhouette and construction
- age, repair, conflict, migration, and adaptation
- population density and daily life
- relationship between built space and landscape
- light, weather, and atmosphere
- cultural distinctions without costume-park shorthand
- manifestation interaction

Weathering follows exposure, maintenance, and use. Architecture is recognized by proportion, rhythm, massing, circulation, joinery, surface behavior, and terrain—not decorative decals.

Use environmental evidence: wear paths, repairs, repurposing, resource movement, boundaries, abandoned/protected areas, hierarchy, and consequences of history.

## Intentional Dissonance

A location may feel out of place due to history, intrusion, isolation, technology, migration, faction, or another canon cause. It must be deliberate, motivated, internally coherent, visually consistent, and clearly contrasted—not random drift.

Manifestations remain local outside true full manifestation and do not erase architecture, geography, weather, or ambient light.

Dark, moody treatments are approved for occasional dungeons and later civilizations, not as the universal palette.
